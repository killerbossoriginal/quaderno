# O Captain! My Captain!
It speaks about the death Abraham Lincoln, after the end of the war (5 days later.)
The civil war has started because Southern states didn't want to free the slaves.

The first part is celebration, the second is mourning for the captain.

For you [...] (r. 10-13), alliteration, rhythm.

Symbols:
1. fearful trip: the war
2. the prize: abolition of slavery, the reunification of america
3. the port: the victory of democracy
4. vessel grim and daring

safe and sound, pale and still, rise up

the present tense prevails, because it's like we are there with the poet.
